Churches are part of a wider system where large landholders pay no land tax.

  • Religious organisations
  • Charities with large land holdings
  • Clubs and associations

The issue isn’t who owns the land. It’s how it’s used.

A simple rule

If land is used for public good → it stays tax-free
If land builds wealth or sits idle → it should be taxed

No loopholes. No special treatment.

End tax exemptions for wealthy institutions. Fund the services NSW depends on.

The Problem

The System in NSW Is Broken.

01

Religious organisations own billions in NSW land and many holdings remain exempt from land tax under current rules.

02

Nurses, teachers, and essential workers are underpaid and understaffed.

03

Everyday Australians carry the tax burden while some large institutions contribute less than comparable entities.

Policy

Priorities

1

Fair Land Tax Reform

End special treatment for large-scale religious and institutional landholdings by applying standard NSW land tax — while protecting genuine places of worship and small community organisations.

  • Tax commercial operations, investment properties, and income-generating assets
  • Set firm thresholds so small organisations remain protected
  • Mandate full transparency with independent oversight and enforcement
2

Gender Equality

End pay gaps and unequal treatment by enforcing clear, measurable standards across workplaces and public life.

  • Enforce equal pay for equal work with mandatory reporting and real penalties
  • Crack down on discrimination, harassment, and unequal treatment
  • Expand access to leadership, education, and economic opportunity for underrepresented groups
Other policies we're working on

Other policies we're working on

3

Buy Back Our Roads

End decades of toll extraction. Return NSW roads to public ownership over time, reducing the financial burden on working families who commute daily.

  • Structured buyback plan using land tax revenue
  • Priority on highest-traffic toll roads
  • No new tolls without a public vote
4

A Fair Share Levy

A state-based levy on large corporations generating $100M+ revenue in NSW while contributing minimally to public infrastructure and services.

  • Targets large-scale revenue extraction
  • Protects small and medium businesses entirely
  • Aligns with existing payroll-style tax models
5

Fix Organ Donation Now

NSW should move to an opt-out organ donation system. If you are on the register, your family has no veto. Your decision is final.

  • Opt-out by default — silence means consent to donate
  • Licence registration is binding — family override is removed
  • Motorbike licence holders are automatically registered as donors
  • Thousands more lives saved every year through a simple policy change
